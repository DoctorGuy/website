Let's take a look at my fantasy team this year. For the uninitiated, fantasy football is a game where usually 8-16 people 'draft' players I came in third, and I want to use the power of hindsight to get a better idea of what I can do to perform better next year. In my league, we have 10 teams, with each team starting one Quarter back (QB), two Running backs(RB), two Wide Receivers (WR), one Tight End (TE), one Defense/Special Teams (D/ST), and one Kicker (K). We also have a flex spot that could contain either an RB, WR, or TE. We have seven bench spots that can contain any back-ups not already on other teams. We use a half-points per reception scoring format placing a little more weight on position's outside QB.

A little context for my league, we've been playing for 3 years and no one ever wants to trade. I do not know why, but it is what it is, so the draft and waiver wire picks are incredibly important. Draft strategies very wildly, and there is a lot of planning and practicing that go into them, but I decided to do the following:

  • Ignore TE completely and get a rookie
  • Aim for a value pick at QB
  • Grab as many safe WR/RB options as I could as quickly as I could

So using the power of hindsight, and pretending that if I were to make a different pick, I'd essentially swap the new pick with the other team, I decided to test out three strategies. Each of these strategies must work withing the rules of the league, so if I need a TE, even if their is a player with more points, I'll take the TE. In order to avoid having 16 QB's, my new team will be limited to 2 QB's, 5 RB's, 5 WR's, 3 TE's, 3 D/ST, and 3 Kicker's. Even though I think having a back-up kicker is dumb, I will allow it to demonstrate value in the late picks. That being said, the three strategies are:

  1. the player with the highest average score per game.
  2. Pick the highest points in this order: RB/RB/WR/WR/RB/QB/RB/TE/RB/WR/WR/QB/WR/RB/DST/K
  3. Draft Travis Kelce: Then run a modified "Hero RB" pick order TE/RB/WR/WR/RB/QB/RB/RB/WR/RB/WR/QB/WR/RB/K/DST

Strategy One: Highest PPG

Taking the highest average picks and placing them in the starting positions, we see this strategy raises the total average score from 110 to 126. A worth while upgrade but with some obvious caveats. First, Jalen Hurts is a wild First round pick followed by another QB, it really misses out on some key opportunities in each round. It also only works because I know their scores. Several of these players went well above their expected seasons, so to hit on every player, is only possible because we know the outcome. We also see just how hard getting a good TE was last year. This TE was only picked because the algorithm forced a TE pick. While it's a technical improvement, I think it could be improved on by prioritizing certain picks, both as it's more realistic, and there is more value for certain roles in later rounds.

Strategy Two: Predetermined ordered picks: Tried and True RB-Heavy

Injuries happen. Especially to your over-utilized yet fantasy star running backs. Derek Henry, Alvin Kamara, and Christian McCaffery have produced well but missed a significant amount of time over the last 3 seasons. An RB heavy approach allows us to fill in the gaps when they miss some time and purely anecdotally, I feel they produce more consistent results than a WR who might not see a target due to double coverage.
